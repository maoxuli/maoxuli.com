Case study: A media player based on ActiveX controls

Sep 2010 - Dec 2010, The George Washington University, Washington DC

This is a hands-on practice for my coursework at The George Washington University. ActiveX control is a component on Windows system with binary compatible interface. Manufacturers of video decoder usually provide an ActiveX controls as SDK. Based on such ActiveX controls, it is easy to develop a video player that support multiple video formats. In addition, flash is more and more used as a GUI element recently. Adobe flash player also has an ActiveX control distributed with it to support flash playback. In this case study, I demonstrate a media player implementation. It supports playback of video contents in ASF/WMV formats with ActiveX control of Windows Media Player, and RM/RMVB formats with ActiveX control of Real Player. In addition, its entire GUI (all visible elements) is realized with a flash. Playing of flash is done with ActiveX control of Flash Player. Strategy and Factory design patterns are used in this case to establish a extendable framework for supporting of new types of ActiveX controls, hence support playback of new formats of media.

Please find source code and documents at Github:
https://github.com/maoxuli/mplayer
