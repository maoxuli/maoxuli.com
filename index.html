Biography

Maoxu Li is a senior software developer with more than 10 years work experience. He used to be a programmer, a software architect, a team leader, and a project director in China. His areas of interests include networking and distributed computing, computer and network security, multimedia application over internet, but more specifically peer-to-peer (P2P) content delivery & distribution networks. He enjoys coding and is fluent in C/C++.

His undergraduate study majored in Communications Engineering and his first job in 1997-1998 was on a Telecommunications Management Networks (TMN) system. These backgrounds had him deeply understood the principles of modern communications and computer networks, including the the layered reference models and the relevant protocols. After that he started working as a software developer. It was also the clear thinking of layered architecture in system analysis and design that benefited him greatly.

His early work (from 2001 to 2005) was a set of projects to develop video surveillance softwares used in a integrated security system. In the recent years (after 2006), he focused on peer-to-peer (P2P) applications for internet video streaming. He worked as a project director and software architect, led teams developing commercial video streaming systems, including a peer-to-peer (P2P) video streaming system and the operating support system (OSS).

Currently, he is pursuing a M.S. degree in Computer Science at The George Washington University as while as working on an open source project PPEngine to explore the possibility of Internet working in a peer-to-peer (P2P) fashion.

In working, Maoxu Li is a team player with superb interpersonal and communicational skills particularly in conveying complex technical information in a clear and convincing manner to diverse audiences. In daily life, he is a versatile person with widespread interests in all kinds of DIY, from computer hardware and household electronic appliance to cooking and gardening.
