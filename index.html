Biography

Maoxu Li is a senior software developer with more than 10 years' work experience. He used to be a programmer, a software architect, a team leader, and a project director in China. His areas of interests include peer-to-peer (P2P), distributed systems, self organising networks, but more specifically Autonomic Content Distribution Networks. He enjoys coding and is fluent in C/C++.

His undergraduate study majored in Communications Engineering and his first job in 1997 was on a Telecommunications Management Networks (TMN) system. These backgrounds gave him a much deep understanding of the principles of modern communications and computer networks, especially of the the layered reference models and the relevant protocols. One years later, he started working as a software developer. It was still the clear thinking of layered architecture in system analysis and design that benefited him greatly.

From 2001 to 2005, he worked on a projects to develop video surveillance systems and there he specialized in transmitting video over Internet and accumulated abundant experience in network programming. In 2006, he went to the field of peer-to-peer (P2P) networking. He led teams developing a peer-to-peer video streaming system and the operating support system (OSS).

Currently, he is working on an open source project, PPEngine, to explore the possibility of Internet that works in a peer-to-peer (P2P) fashion.

In working, Maoxu Li is a team player with superb interpersonal and communicational skills particularly in conveying complex technical information in a clear and convincing manner to diverse audiences. In actual life, he is a versatile person with widespread interests in all kinds of DIY, such as computer hardware, household electronic appliance, and even cooking and gardening.
