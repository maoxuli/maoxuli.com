FireWind: An Peer-to-Peer video streaming system

Jun 2006 - Apr 2009, QQ Technology Inc., Beijing China

Incorporated in 2001 in Beijing, QQ Technology obtained the investment from IDG Technology Venture Investment and Mitsui Ventures. QQ Technology designs and develops solutions driven by the market for the communications and telecommunications industry, and provides networking service providers and enterprises with state-of-the-art products as well as services. The company is the first to deliver carrier-grade DPI (Deep Packet Inspection) products into the Chinese market in May, 2005. I joined QQ Technology in June, 2006 as a Project Director and Software Architect to lead the software development for an Internet video streaming system.

FireWind is an Internet video stream system based on peer-to-peer (P2P) architecture.

  • Design a peer-to-peer (P2P) video streaming system and manage the development.
  • Manage the development of a web-based operational support system (OSS) for online video and advertisements.
  • Manage the development of a web portal for WebTV (WebVOD) application.
  • Manage the development of a media center software running on HTPC (Home Theater PC).
Languages: 80% C++, 20% C
Operating systems: Windows XP/Vista, Linux
DB and Tools: MySQL, Visual Studio
Techniques: Thread, Socket, Client/Server, ActiveX, OOD/OOP

GoldenEye: A digital video surveillance system

Nov 2002 - Dec 2004, Golden Eye Technology Co. Ltd., Beijing China

Golden Eye Technology (currently Golden Vision Technology) developed the first PC-based DVR (Digital Video Recorder) system in China in 1997 and successfully used in a digital video surveillance system. In the following years, the company was always the leader and promoter in the market of digital video surveillance in China. I joined Golden Eye in November, 2002 as a C++ programmer in a software development team to maintain and improve the software of GoldenEye™ series digital video surveillance systems.

DVR is the core part of the digital video surveillance system.

  • I debug the software and fix bugs driven by testing reports.
  • I modify or redesign software modules driven by requirements update.
  • I restructured the client software into an ActiveX control to support remote video surveillance on B/S（Browser/Server) architecture.
Languages: 80% C++, 20% C
Operating systems: Windows 2000/XP
Libraries and tools: STL, MFC, Win32 API, Winsock, DivX/Xvid, OpenCV, Visual C++ 6.0
Techniques: Thread, Socket, Client/Server, ActiveX
